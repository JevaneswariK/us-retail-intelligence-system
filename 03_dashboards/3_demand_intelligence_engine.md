#  Dashboard 3 — Demand Intelligence Engine  
### Category Performance, Efficiency & Distribution

## Overview

The Demand Intelligence Engine focuses on category-level performance in the retail sector.  
It analyzes efficiency, variability, and distribution to understand how different categories contribute to overall revenue.

## Problem Statement

All categories do not perform equally. The challenge is to:

- Identify which categories drive revenue  
- Measure how consistent their performance is  
- Understand how revenue is distributed across categories  

## Approach

- Calculated category-level revenue and dispersion metrics  
- Used Coefficient of Variation (CV) to measure consistency  
- Applied percentile-based metric (P10) to assess downside risk  
- Segmented categories into Stable, Balanced, and High Variability groups  
- Used Lorenz Curve to analyze distribution inequality  

## Key Metrics

- **Average Revenue (Top Categories)** → Baseline category performance  
- **Revenue Dispersion (CV)** → Variation across categories  
- **P10 Revenue** → Downside risk level  
- **Revenue Efficiency Ratio** → Top vs median category performance  

## Key Findings

- Revenue is concentrated in a few key categories  
- High-performing categories show better stability and efficiency  
- Some categories have high volatility with inconsistent returns  
- Lorenz curve indicates moderate inequality in distribution  

## Business Interpretation

This analysis helps stakeholders to:

- Optimize category-level strategies  
- Identify high-efficiency segments  
- Balance risk across categories  
- Allocate resources more effectively
